Constant Dullaart

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Constant Dullaart (born 1979) is a Dutch conceptual artist, media artist, internet artist, and curator. He is best known for exploring the political and aesthetic structures of the internet, often using digital platforms as both subject and medium. His work critiques corporate control over online space, visibility, and behavior.

Early life and education

Dullaart studied at the Gerrit Rietveld Academy and the Rijksakademie van Beeldende Kunsten in Amsterdam. Initially trained in video art, he later transitioned toward internet-based works, engaging critically with the infrastructures of digital culture.

Artistic practice

Working primarily with the internet as his material, Dullaart’s projects investigate how online systems—such as algorithms, social media platforms, and interfaces—mediate perception and shape human interaction. His works often manipulate familiar web environments to challenge viewers’ trust in digital “normalcy.”

In 2015, Dullaart received the Prix Net Art for his contributions to internet-based art.

Major themes

Recurring themes in Dullaart’s practice include authenticity, algorithmic bias, digital labor, and the aesthetics of visibility. His works frequently expose the underlying mechanisms that govern online experience.

Notable projects

  • The Disagreeing Internet – a parody of Google pages featuring “disagree” buttons, emphasizing the illusion of user agency in online spaces.
  • Jennifer in Paradise – a reinterpretation of the first image ever manipulated in Photoshop, questioning the origins of digital image culture.
  • High Retention, Slow Delivery – a performance piece in which the artist purchased fake social media followers to explore the commodification of attention.
