Context: Net Art and digital culture

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Dullaart’s work is situated within the broader movement of Net Art, a form of art created for and experienced on the internet. Emerging in the 1990s, Net Art often employed web browsers, HTML, and code as artistic tools. Artists in this field examined the social, political, and aesthetic dimensions of online environments.

Belonging to a generation that grew up alongside the internet, Dullaart’s perspective is characterized by an insider critique—his works both inhabit and distort the digital world to make its invisible structures perceptible.
